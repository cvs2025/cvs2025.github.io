Why This Conversation Matters Today

Deepfakes and synthetic media now influence elections, reputations, and markets in India, creating an urgent need to understand what the new rules demand from platforms, creators, and businesses. The 2026 amendments to the IT Act have introduced India’s first formal definition and regulatory framework for Synthetically Generated Information (SGI), a category that now sits at the centre of public discourse on AI-generated deepfakes, misinformation, and platform responsibility.

As platforms, creators, businesses, and users grapple with mandatory labelling, compressed takedown timelines, and uncertain liability boundaries, the need for clarity has never been more urgent. This session brings together legal interpretation, policy context, and practical implications to help stakeholders understand what the law says, and what it leaves open.

Sachin Dhawan is one of India’s most respected voices in the digital policy ecosystem. A dual-qualified technology lawyer with deep expertise in data protection and content regulation, Sachin brings over 17 years of experience across academia, industry, and public policy.
